Why ERPNext fails

The problems every business faces before us.

ERPNext is powerful. But most implementations fail — not because of the software, but because of how it's implemented. Here's what goes wrong, and exactly how we fix it.

The Problem
Our Fix
01
No fixed timeline — projects drag for months

Most vendors quote "8–12 months" with no fixed end date. Every delay costs you money, staff patience, and confidence. Businesses go live 2 years later — or never.

"Our previous vendor started in January 2022. By December 2023 we still weren't live. We called these guys in February 2024 and went live in April."
— Director, Manufacturing co. Bengaluru
02
Scope keeps expanding — invoice keeps growing

You sign a contract for ₹4L. Six months later you've paid ₹11L in "change requests" for features that should have been included. Scope creep kills ERP projects.

"Every week there was a new invoice for something that 'wasn't in scope'. We had no idea what we'd signed up for."
— CFO, Trading company, Chennai
03
Custom code written — then no one can maintain it

Vendors write thousands of lines of custom code to "fit" your process. Every ERPNext update breaks it. You're now dependent on that vendor forever — at whatever price they charge.

"After they left, our system broke on every update. Nobody in our team could touch the code. We were hostages."
— IT Head, Retail chain, Hyderabad
04
Training is a 2-hour demo — your team is lost

A generic walkthrough of menus is not training. Your accounts team, warehouse staff, and sales team all have different needs. One session fits nobody.

"They gave us a 2-hour Zoom call and said we were trained. Half my team didn't even know how to raise a purchase order."
— Operations Manager, Logistics firm, Mumbai
05
Go-live day is chaos — then they disappear

Data migrates wrong, first transactions fail, nobody picks up the phone. The vendor calls it "hypercare" but you haven't heard from them in 3 weeks. The first 30 days are the most critical — and you're alone.

"On go-live day our stock levels were wrong, our invoices weren't printing, and our vendor's phone was off. It was a disaster."
— MD, Wholesale distributor, Pune
How we solve this

Built differently, from day one, on purpose.

Every problem on the left is something we've seen, documented, and systematically eliminated from our process.

Locked go-live date — signed on Day 2 Not a range. A specific date. In the contract.
Fixed price — no change requests Scope is defined upfront. What's in is in. Nothing added.
Zero custom code — ever Standard ERPNext only. Every update works. No hostage situation.
Role-based training — not a demo Each team gets their own session with their own data.
30-day hypercare — we don't disappear Same-day response. Weekly health checks. Included, not billed.
